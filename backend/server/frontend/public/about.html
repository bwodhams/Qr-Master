About QR Codes for Good

Every year, the University of Arizona College of Engineering has a Senior Design project for graduating students. Projects are brought to the U of A seniors from real companies, and multi-disciplinary teams are created.

Our project was brought forth by Microsoft, and was dubbed “QR Codes for Good”. Team 18080 was born, and recruited Muneeb Ahmed, an Electrical and Computer Engineer, Amanda Chesin, an Electrical and Computer Engineer, Corey Miner, an Electrical and Computer Engineer, Sanarya Salah, a Systems Engineer, and Benjamin Wodhams, an Electrical and Computer Engineer. Sanarya Salah served as the project lead, Amanda Chesin served as the project manager, and Corey Miner served as the purchasing lead.

The project scope was described as this: nowadays people do not carry cash around with them, but they do carry their phones. When people see homeless people on the streets, get their car parked at a valet, or have the door held open for them by their building’s doorman, they want to help them out, but are unable to do so without cash. QR Codes for Good can help do that.

The purpose of this project is to create a payment transaction system that generates QR codes on a mobile application and website with a specific amount of money decided by the receiver and can be adjusted by the payer.

The app interface was created with React Native with an Azure Database securely storing all information. Payments are processed with the Stripe Payment Processor.
